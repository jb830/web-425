Johann Sebastian Bach (1685-1750)

A German composer and musician of the Baroque period known for his complex compositions and mastery of harmony. Bach composed over 1,000 works including the Brandenburg Concertos and the Well-Tempered Clavier. Bach served as Kapellmeister in Kothen and as cantor at St. Thomas Church in Leipzig where he is buried. His music, which spans sacred and secular genres forms a significant part of classical music history.
